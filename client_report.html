Client Report

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

The client gave us the images we were supposed to use. I followed it from there, including using the wireframe granted to us.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?

We mainly communicated in-person, sometimes there would be messages left between us on this page; for reference, those have been mostly removed.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

Yes, he liked what we did and he didn't have any major complaints.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?

First of all, I would establish more defined communication protocols, as just meeting in person wasn't terribly consistent. Secondly, I would try to have more frequent check-ins to ensure that the project is on track and aligns with my vision. Lastly, I would provide more detailed feedback throughout the development process to help guide the team.

How did you communicate with your team lead? Was there any issues?

We communicated mostly in-person, and there were no major issues. However, I think having a more structured communication plan could have improved our collaboration. That said, I did get one of the pages quite late, as the invitations for whatever reason were not working.

Did you know what was expected of you for each site?

For the first one, yes. The second one, not so much, as I was not given the wireframe until quite late in the process. Even then, there were quite a few hiccups in the process.

Were you able to contribute to your sites?

Yes, I did my part in working with the team to ensure that the content and images were appropriate for the site. I also provided feedback on the design and layout to help improve the overall user experience.

Did you run into any issues with GitHub and adding pages to your site?

Shockingly, that was the only thing that consistently worked. I had no issues with GitHub at all.
